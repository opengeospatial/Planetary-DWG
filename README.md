# About the Planetary DWG
The Planetary Domain Working Group (DWG) is a community of experts and enthusiasts interested in planetary science, exploration, implementation and observation. Our mission is to foster collaboration and knowledge-sharing among stakeholders in the field and to support the development of standards and best practices for planetary data, models, and tools. Our focus is on the scientific exploration of the Solar System and beyond, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

More information about the Planetary DWG:

- [charter of the Planetary DWG](https://portal.ogc.org/files/?artifact_id=99970)
- mailing list: *planetary.dwg@lists.ogc.org*
- subscribe to the mailing list : *https://lists.ogc.org/mailman/listinfo/Planetary.DWG*


## Objectives

- Identify and prioritize key scientific questions and challenges in planetary science
- Promote interoperability and standardization of planetary data and models
- Facilitate collaboration among diverse communities and stakeholders in planetary science
- Develop and maintain a repository of best practices, tools, and resources for planetary exploration and observation

## Membership

The Planetary DWG is open to anyone interested in planetary science, exploration, and observation. Our members include scientists, engineers, students and enthusiasts from academia, government, industry, and non-profit organizations.

## Activities

Our activities include:

- Contributing to the development of standards and best practices for planetary data and models
- Developing and maintaining a repository of best practices, tools, and resources for planetary exploration and observation
- Collaborating with other OGC domain working groups and standards organizations on interoperability and standardization initiatives
- Organizing workshops, webinars, and other events on key topics in planetary science and exploration

# Planetary DWG Resources and Tools

The Planetary DWG maintains a repository of best practices, tools, and resources for planetary exploration and observation. This repository is available on the Planetary DWG wiki and includes:

- Standards and guidelines for planetary data and models
- Software and tools for data processing, mapping, and visualization
- Datasets and sample data for testing and validation
- Publications and reports on best practices and case studies

The Planetary DWG also collaborates with other organizations and initiatives in the field to promote interoperability and standardization of planetary data and models.

# Planetary DWG Discussion Forum

The Planetary DWG maintains a discussion forum on the OGC Community mailing list platform. This forum is open to all members and interested parties and is intended for discussions related to the work of the Planetary DWG. Members can post questions, share their expertise and knowledge, and discuss ongoing initiatives and projects.

The discussion forum is moderated by the Co-Chairs of the Planetary DWG, and all posts are subject to the OGC Code of Conduct. Members are encouraged to use the mailing list to stay up-to-date with the latest developments in the field and to connect with other stakeholders in planetary science and exploration.

# Planetary DWG Tasks and Priorities

The Planetary DWG has identified several priority areas for its work, including:

- Developing and maintaining standards and guidelines for planetary data and models
- Promoting interoperability and data sharing among stakeholders in the field
- Collaborating with other OGC domain/standard working groups and standards organizations on interoperability and standardization initiatives
- Organizing workshops and webinars to promote knowledge-sharing and collaboration among diverse communities in planetary science and exploration
- Maintaining a repository of best practices, tools, and resources for planetary exploration and observation

To achieve these objectives, the Planetary DWG has established several task groups and initiatives, which are open to all members and interested parties. The Planetary DWG also welcomes suggestions and proposals for new initiatives and projects that align with its mission and objectives.

1. Standards and guidelines: This group would focus on developing and maintaining standards and guidelines for planetary data and models. It would work closely with other OGC domain working groups and standards organizations to ensure interoperability and consistency across the field such as IPDA, IVOA, DOI

2. Data interoperability: This group would focus on promoting data interoperability and sharing among stakeholders in the field of planetary science and exploration. It would work on developing data exchange formats, data access protocols, and other tools and technologies that enable data sharing and integration such as STAC extensions

3. Tools and software: This group would focus on developing and maintaining software and tools for data processing, mapping, and visualization in planetary science and exploration. It would work on identifying and testing existing tools and technologies, as well as developing new ones as needed.

4. Workshops and webinars: This group would focus on organizing workshops and webinars to promote knowledge-sharing and collaboration among diverse communities in planetary science and exploration. It would identify relevant topics and speakers, and work on planning and organizing the events.

5. Outreach and education: This group would focus on outreach and education activities to promote the work of the Planetary DWG and raise awareness about the importance of interoperability and data sharing in the field of planetary science and exploration. It would work on developing outreach materials, organizing public events, and engaging with relevant stakeholders in the field.
